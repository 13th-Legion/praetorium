## Key Findings

### Threat Environment

The Corpus Christi metro area presents a **low-to-moderate threat environment** for conservative political organizations. As a smaller metropolitan area compared to major Texas cities, organized activist presence is limited, with sporadic protest activity often tied to national events or local environmental/immigration issues. Proximity to the U.S.-Mexico border, Naval Air Station Corpus Christi, and critical port/refinery infrastructure introduces unique vulnerabilities.

**Primary Threat Actors:**
- **Environmental activist groups** — Focused on opposing port expansions, refineries, and desalination projects; e.g., groups protesting Exxon SABIC plastics plant and industrial pollution in refinery-adjacent neighborhoods.
- **Immigration rights organizations** — Including LULAC and local chapters protesting ICE raids and border policies; participated in "No Kings" anti-deportation protests.
- **Civil rights groups** — NAACP, Mosaic Project (LGBT rights), and student activists at TAMUCC; occasional BLM-affiliated demonstrations.
- **Decentralized left-leaning networks** — Limited evidence of organized Antifa presence; sporadic appearances at Trump rallies and national protest events.

**Secondary Considerations:**
- Border proximity may amplify immigration-related unrest.
- Naval Air Station and port infrastructure could attract targeted protests or security incidents.
- Minimal organized militant left-wing activity; most activism is peaceful and issue-specific.

### Priority Recommendations

1. **Infrastructure Monitoring:** Maintain vigilance around NAS Corpus Christi, port facilities, and refineries; coordinate with local law enforcement for early warning of protests.
2. **Event Planning:** Select venues away from high-profile sites like Bayfront or City Hall; prepare for small-scale disruptions.
3. **Community Engagement:** Build relationships with local civil rights groups to mitigate misunderstandings.
4. **OPSEC for Border Areas:** Enhance security protocols for activities near South Texas corridors.
5. **Intelligence Sharing:** Leverage state networks for monitoring national protest trends that may spillover to Corpus Christi.
