## 6.2 Reactive Triggers

These events can trigger rapid mobilization with little warning:

### High-Impact Triggers

**Police Shootings / Use of Force:**
- Any police killing of Black/Latino individual in DFW
- Particularly if video emerges
- 24-72 hour mobilization window typical

**Court Decisions:**
- Supreme Court rulings on abortion, immigration, LGBTQ+ rights
- High-profile criminal verdicts (police officers, political figures)
- Local court decisions in significant cases

**Federal Immigration Enforcement:**
- ICE raids (especially workplaces, schools)
- High-profile deportations
- Detention center incidents
- Policy announcements

**Political Violence:**
- Attacks on left-wing figures/groups
- Mass shootings (especially with political/racial motivation)
- White supremacist incidents

### Moderate-Impact Triggers

**Legislative Actions:**
- Texas legislature sessions (anti-trans bills, abortion restrictions, etc.)
- Federal policy announcements

**Local Government Actions:**
- City council votes on policing, homeless policy, development
- School board decisions (book bans, DEI policy)

**National Political Events:**
- Presidential speeches, rallies in Texas
- Cabinet member visits
- Major national protests with local solidarity actions

## 6.3 Client-Specific Triggers

Conservative political organization events may specifically trigger response when:

1. **High-profile speakers** — National figures attract more opposition
2. **Controversial topics** — Immigration, LGBTQ+ issues, election integrity
3. **Public venues** — Visible, accessible locations easier to protest
4. **Media coverage** — Pre-event publicity enables mobilization
5. **Symbolic timing** — Events near sensitive dates compound risk
