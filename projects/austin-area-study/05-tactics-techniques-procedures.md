# Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTPs)

## 5.1 Protest & Direct Action Tactics

### Standard Protest Tactics

**Marches & Rallies:**
- Organized at Capitol, UT campus, Republic Square
- Chants, signs, speeches
- Often permitted but can go unpermitted

**Civil Disobedience:**
- Sit-ins, die-ins, building occupations
- Blocking entrances to government buildings
- Designed for arrests and media

**Noise Demonstrations:**
- Drums, megaphones outside targeted venues
- Used to disrupt legislative hearings

### Escalated Tactics

**Property Disruption:**
- Graffiti on public buildings
- Vandalism during larger protests
- Rare arson attempts

**Direct Confrontation:**
- Blocking paths, verbal harassment
- Pushing in crowds
- Use of umbrellas, signs as barriers

**Armed Presence:**
- Less common than in other areas, but historical groups like Red Guard carried
- Open carry legal, but rare in protests

## 5.2 Counter-Protest Dynamics

**Phase 1 — Intelligence Gathering:**
- Monitor conservative event announcements via social media

**Phase 2 — Mobilization:**
- Calls on Instagram, Facebook, Signal
- Coalition with student groups

**Phase 3 — Deployment:**
- Gather at venue perimeters
- Chanting to disrupt

**Phase 4 — Confrontation:**
- Attempts to enter or block events
- Potential clashes if opposed

**Phase 5 — Documentation:**
- Filming for doxxing
- Social media amplification

## 5.3 Doxxing & Online Targeting

**Methods:**
- OSINT from event photos, social profiles
- Publishing on Twitter, Reddit

**Consequences:**
- Harassment campaigns
- Employer contacts

**Protective Measures:**
- See recommendations.

## 5.4 Physical Security Concerns

### Armed Groups

- Historical (Red Guard); current groups less armed
- Risk in escalations

### Projectiles

- Pepper spray, fireworks
- Thrown objects in crowds

### Vehicle Tactics

- Convoys to protests
- Blocking roads
