# Threat Landscape

## Overview

The Houston metropolitan area presents a complex threat environment shaped by its size, diversity, economic importance, and historical significance as George Floyd's hometown. The region hosts active left-wing organizing infrastructure, including the largest PSL chapter social media presence in Texas, as well as right-wing extremist groups.

Uniquely among Texas metros, Houston also faces elevated risk from Islamist-inspired terrorism, with multiple Houston-area residents connected to recent attacks and plots.

---

## Left-Wing Threat Actors

### Tier 1: Primary Concern

#### Party for Socialism and Liberation — Houston

**Threat Assessment: ELEVATED**

PSL Houston represents the most significant organized left-wing presence in the region:

- **Scale:** 15,000+ Instagram followers — largest of any Texas socialist organization
- **Infrastructure:** Opening physical community organizing center in early 2026
- **Activity Level:** Highly active in protest organizing, particularly anti-Israel/pro-Palestinian events
- **Documented Incidents:** ADL documented December 2024 protest at Houston synagogue where attendees were harassed with antisemitic slurs ("baby killers," "Zionist pigs")

**Demographic Profile:**
- Mixed gender (~55% male, ~45% female/non-binary)
- Diverse racial composition reflecting Houston demographics (~40% Hispanic, ~30% White, ~20% Black)
- Young adult membership (18-35 primary)
- Significant Spanish-speaking membership

**Indicators to Monitor:**

- Opening and activities at new community center
- Coalition building with other left groups
- Targeting of specific venues or organizations

## Lessons Learned

### From 2020 Protests

- **Coordination with city leadership reduces violence:** Memorial march with Mayor Turner was peaceful vs. initial protests
- **Celebrity/influencer involvement matters:** Trae tha Truth and Bun B provided credible organizing leadership
- **HPD use of force creates legal liability:** Multiple lawsuits followed mounted officer incident
- **Houston can mobilize massive crowds:** 60,000+ at memorial march

### From Terrorism Incidents

- **Local radicalization is occurring:** Multiple Houston residents involved in Islamist-inspired violence
- **Houses of worship are targets:** Churches, synagogues, temples all affected
- **Vehicle attacks are a methodology:** Bourbon Street attacker from Houston used rental truck

### From Right-Wing Activity

- **Houston was early organizing ground:** Patriot Front's first appearance here
- **Propaganda operations ongoing:** Flyer distribution continues
- **Less direct confrontation than DFW:** Fewer armed standoffs at events
# Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTPs)

## Left-Wing TTPs

### Protest Organization

#### PSL Houston

- **Mobilization:** Instagram/Facebook announcements; coalition outreach
- **Timing:** Often tied to national/international events (Israel-Gaza, police killings)
- **Locations:** Government buildings, corporate HQs, symbolic locations
- **Signage:** Professional-quality banners with PSL branding

## Event-Specific Risk Factors

### Factors That Increase Risk

1. **Controversial speaker:** Speakers known to left (turning point, etc.)
2. **Jewish affiliation:** Events connected to Jewish community
3. **Timing:** Events near high-risk dates
4. **Location:** Downtown, near government buildings
5. **Publicity:** High-profile media coverage
6. **Size:** Larger events draw more attention

### Factors That Decrease Risk

1. **Private venue:** Controlled access
2. **Low publicity:** Limited advance notice
3. **Non-controversial agenda:** Routine political activity
4. **Strong security:** Visible professional security
5. **HPD presence:** Law enforcement coordination
6. **Off-peak timing:** Avoiding high-risk dates

---

## Residual Risk Statement

Even with comprehensive security measures, residual risk remains:

- **Lone actor attacks:** Cannot be fully prevented
- **Online harassment:** Difficult to stop entirely
- **Protest activity:** Protected First Amendment activity
- **Reputation attacks:** Outside security scope

Organizations must accept some level of risk when operating in politically contested environments. The goal is risk management, not risk elimination.
# Recommendations

## Executive Summary

Based on the threat landscape analysis, the following recommendations are provided for conservative political organizations operating in the Houston metropolitan area.

---

## Security Recommendations

### Event Security

#### Pre-Event Planning

1. **Threat Assessment:** Conduct event-specific threat assessment 2-4 weeks prior
2. **Venue Selection:**
   - Avoid venues near synagogues or Jewish community centers (PSL targeting)
   - Prefer locations with controlled access and parking
   - Consider indoor venues to limit protest proximity
   - Verify venue security capabilities
3. **HPD Coordination:** Notify Houston Police Department for events over 100 attendees
4. **Intelligence Monitoring:** Monitor PSL Houston and Houston DSA social media for counter-protest announcements

#### Day-of Security

1. **Professional Security:** Engage licensed security personnel with crowd management experience
2. **Access Control:** Credential verification; bag checks; no-backpack policies
3. **Perimeter Security:** Establish buffer zones between event and public areas
4. **Medical Preparedness:** On-site medical personnel for events over 200 attendees
5. **Evacuation Planning:** Multiple exit routes; designated rally points

#### Vehicle Attack Mitigation

Given Bourbon Street attack methodology:
- Vehicle barriers at outdoor events
- Standoff distance from vehicle access points
- Awareness of rental truck threats

### Personnel Security (OPSEC)

#### Leadership Protection

1. **Personal Information:** Minimize public exposure of home addresses, family details
2. **Social Media:** Review and restrict personal account privacy settings
3. **Travel Security:** Vary routes; awareness of surveillance
4. **Residence Security:** Consider home security upgrades for high-profile individuals

#### Doxxing Prevention

1. **Domain Privacy:** WHOIS protection for organizational websites
2. **Corporate Records:** Use registered agent services
3. **Event Registration:** Avoid publishing attendee lists
4. **Staff Training:** Awareness of social engineering tactics

## Operational Recommendations

### Event Planning

#### Low-Risk Events (Internal meetings, small gatherings)

- Standard venue security
- Limited public announcement
- Basic access control

#### Medium-Risk Events (Public forums, candidate appearances)

- Professional security team (4-6 personnel)
- HPD notification
- Social media monitoring 1 week prior
- Credentialed access

#### High-Risk Events (Major rallies, controversial speakers)

- Enhanced security team (10+ personnel)
- HPD coordination and possible presence
- Counter-surveillance
- Legal observer/documentation
- Media management plan
- Medical team on-site
- Vehicle barriers (outdoor events)

### Incident Response

#### Protest Disruption

1. Do not engage with protesters
2. Document via video (discreetly)
3. Allow security to manage perimeter
4. Have spokesperson ready for media
5. Continue event if safely possible

#### Physical Confrontation

1. Immediate security response
2. Call 911
3. Evacuate if necessary
4. Preserve evidence
5. File police report
6. Engage legal counsel

#### Doxxing/Online Attack

1. Document all threats
2. Report to platforms
3. Report credible threats to FBI
4. Consider temporary social media blackout
5. Engage cybersecurity professionals if needed

- Counter-protesters have First Amendment rights on public property

- Photograph any damage

- Verify venue liability coverage
- Consider event-specific insurance
- Have legal counsel on retainer
