# Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTPs)

## Left-Wing TTPs

### Protest Organization

#### PSL Houston

- **Mobilization:** Instagram/Facebook announcements; coalition outreach
- **Timing:** Often tied to national/international events (Israel-Gaza, police killings)
- **Locations:** Government buildings, corporate HQs, symbolic locations
- **Signage:** Professional-quality banners with PSL branding
- **Media:** Active social media documentation; press releases

#### Antifa Networks

- **Mobilization:** Encrypted messaging (Signal, Telegram)
- **Anonymity:** Bloc tactics (black clothing, masks, goggles)
- **Counter-protest:** Arriving to oppose right-wing events
- **Documentation:** May photograph/film opposition for doxxing
- **De-escalation:** Sometimes, depending on chapter philosophy
- **Direct Action:** Property damage, confrontation (in some cases)

### Direct Action Tactics

| Tactic | Description | Groups |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Banner drops | Hanging banners from overpasses/buildings | PSL, antifa |
| Highway blockades | Blocking traffic for visibility | BLM affiliates |
| Building occupation | Taking over public spaces | Rare in Houston |
| Doxxing | Publishing personal info of opponents | Antifa networks |
| Noise demonstrations | Disrupting events with sound | Various |

### Communication Methods

- **Public:** Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook
- **Semi-public:** Action Network, event pages
- **Private:** Signal, Telegram, encrypted email
- **Coordination:** Slack, Discord (for organizing)

## Right-Wing TTPs

### Proud Boys

- **Flash mobs:** Rapid assembly at events
- **Confrontational:** Willing to engage physically
- **Documentation:** Film everything for propaganda/legal defense
- **Social media:** Active promotion of actions

### Patriot Front

- **Propaganda:** Flyer distribution, banner drops, sticker campaigns
- **Flash marches:** Uniformed marches with shields
- **Anonymity:** Masks, no personal identification
- **Media control:** Carefully choreographed imagery

### This Is Texas Freedom Force

- **Armed presence:** Open carry at events
- **Counter-protest:** Opposing left-wing demonstrations
- **Monument defense:** Protecting Confederate/historical sites
- **Border activity:** Participation in border vigilante operations

## Mutual Aid & Support Networks

### Left-Wing Infrastructure

Houston has robust mutual aid networks developed through:
- Hurricane Harvey (2017) response
- COVID-19 mutual aid
- George Floyd protest support

**Key Organizations:**
- Relief Gang
- PSL Houston (opening community center)
- Various anarchist collectives

**Services Provided:**
- Legal support (jail support, legal observers)
- Medical support (street medics)

- Mutual aid builds community support/legitimacy

## Escalation Indicators

### Signs of Potential Violence

1. **Social media threats:** Direct threats against event/attendees
2. **Out-of-town mobilization:** Groups traveling from other cities
3. **Equipment preparation:** Discussions of shields, helmets, weapons
4. **Rhetoric escalation:** "By any means necessary," "shut it down"
5. **Counter-event announced:** Formal counter-protest at same time/place

### De-escalation Factors

1. **Police presence:** Large visible police deployment
2. **Physical separation:** Barriers between groups
3. **Weather:** Extreme heat/rain reduces turnout
4. **Competing events:** Other activities drawing attention
5. **Leadership messaging:** Calls for peaceful protest
