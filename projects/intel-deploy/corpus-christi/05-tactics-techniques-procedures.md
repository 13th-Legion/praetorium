# Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTPs)

## 5.1 Protest & Direct Action Tactics

### Standard Protest Tactics

**Marches & Rallies:**
- Permitted gatherings at parks, City Hall, Bayfront
- Signs, chants, speeches focusing on issues like environment or immigration
- Generally peaceful and protected activity

**Civil Disobedience:**
- Sit-ins at public hearings
- Blocking access to symbolic sites (rare)
- Designed for media attention

**Noise Demonstrations:**
- Used at City Council meetings or industrial sites
- Drums, megaphones for disruption

### Escalated Tactics

**Limited in CC:**  
Due to smaller scale, escalation is rare. Occasional verbal confrontations at rallies, but no documented property destruction or violence in recent protests.

**Armed Presence:**
- Not observed in local activist groups; Texas open carry possible but uncommon.

---

## 5.2 Counter-Protest Dynamics

For events attracting opposition:

**Phase 1 — Intelligence Gathering:**
- Monitoring social media and public announcements

**Phase 2 — Mobilization:**
- Calls via Facebook, Instagram, local networks
- Coalition with civil rights/environmental groups

**Phase 3 — Deployment:**
- Peaceful counter-demonstrations with signs

**Phase 4 — Confrontation:**
- Low risk; mostly verbal if any

**Phase 5 — Documentation:**
- Social media sharing, potential doxxing (rare)

---

## 5.3 Doxxing & Online Targeting

**Methods:**
- Social media analysis
- Event photography
- Publication on local forums

**Consequences:**
- Harassment or employer contact (infrequent)
- Lower prevalence than in larger metros

---

## 5.4 Physical Security Concerns

### Armed Groups

No documented armed left-wing groups in CC. General Texas carry laws apply.

### Projectiles & Weapons

Rare; occasional use of noise devices or signs as improvised tools.

### Vehicle Tactics

Convoys to protest sites; blocking rare.

### Platforms Used

**Public:**
- Facebook events
- Instagram
- Local news comments

**Semi-Private:**
- Group chats on WhatsApp

**Encrypted:**
- Signal (for organizers)

**OPSEC:**
- Basic anonymity; less sophisticated than urban groups

## 5.7 Legal & Media Tactics

### Legal Support

- ACLU Texas involvement in challenges
- Legal observers at larger protests

### Media Engagement

- Press releases to local outlets (Caller-Times, KRIS)
- Social media for narrative control

### Lawfare

- Permit challenges, lawsuits against industrial projects
- Public records requests for transparency
