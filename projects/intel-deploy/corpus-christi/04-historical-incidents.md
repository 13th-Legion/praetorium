# Historical Incident Analysis (2020–Present)

## Major Incidents

### 2020 George Floyd Protests — Corpus Christi

**Date:** May 30, 2020 (primary); sporadic through summer  
**Location:** Art Center of Corpus Christi, Marina Arts District

**Timeline:**
- **May 30, 2020:** Over 100 participants gathered for Black Lives Matter solidarity protest.
- Peaceful demonstration with signs and speeches focusing on racial justice and police reform.
- No reported arrests or violence.

**Outcomes:**
- Highlighted local support for national BLM movement.
- Led to community discussions on CCPD policies.

**Lessons Learned:**
- Peaceful nature reflects smaller metro dynamics.
- Potential for spillover from national events.

---

### 2020 Naval Air Station Shooting

**Date:** May 21, 2020  
**Location:** Naval Air Station Corpus Christi gate

**Summary:**  
Terrorism-related incident where a gunman opened fire, injuring a security officer before being neutralized. Not a protest but relevant to area security.

**Outcomes:**
- FBI investigation confirmed terrorism links.
- Heightened security at NAS CC.

---

### 2019-2020 ICE Raid Protests

**Date:** Various, peaking 2019-2020  
**Location:** Downtown areas, near federal buildings

**Summary:**  
Protests against ICE immigration raids and detention policies, organized by local immigration rights groups.

**Outcomes:**
- Peaceful solidarity events joining national movement.
- Increased local awareness of border issues.

---

### 2024 Desalination Plant Protests

**Date:** June 2024  
**Location:** City Hall, public hearings

**Summary:**  
Environmental groups protested permits for Inner Harbor desalination plant, citing ecosystem impacts on Corpus Christi Bay.

**Outcomes:**
- Public testimony and rallies; some permits challenged legally.
- Ongoing tension with industrial development.

---

### 2025 "No Kings" Protests

**Date:** June 14, 2025 and October 18, 2025  
**Location:** Cole Park (June); Water's Edge Park (October)

**Timeline:**
- **June 14, 2025:** Protest against authoritarian policies, coordinated with national events; permitted and peaceful.
- **October 18, 2025:** Thousands lined Shoreline Boulevard for anti-deportation, free speech rally.

**Outcomes:**
- Largest recent protests in CC; no violence reported.
- Demonstrated mobilization capacity for immigration issues.

**Lessons Learned:**
- Large crowds possible in tourist areas.
- Border proximity amplifies immigration-related activism.

---

## Incident Pattern Analysis

### Escalation Factors

Incidents are more likely to escalate when:
1. **National Triggers:** Tied to immigration policies or environmental decisions.
2. **Symbolic Locations:** Bayfront, City Hall, NAS gates.
3. **Group Coordination:** Coalitions form for larger events.

### Geographic Patterns

| Location Type | Incident Frequency | Severity |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Downtown/Bayfront | MODERATE | LOW |
| City Hall | LOW-MODERATE | LOW |
| NAS/Port Areas | LOW | MODERATE |
| TAMUCC Campus | LOW | LOW |

### Seasonal Patterns

- **Spring/Summer:** Peak for outdoor protests (e.g., June "No Kings").
- **Fall:** Tied to national elections or policies.
- **Winter:** Reduced activity due to weather.
