# Threat Landscape

## 3.1 Left-Wing Activist Groups (Primary Focus)

### Environmental Activist Networks

**Overview:**  
In Corpus Christi, environmental activism centers on opposition to port expansions, refinery pollution, and desalination projects. Groups are decentralized but coordinate through coalitions like the Corpus Area for Positive Environment (CAPE).

**Key Characteristics:**
- Focus on legal challenges, public hearings, and peaceful protests
- Concerns over health impacts in refinery-adjacent neighborhoods (e.g., Hillcrest)
- Opposition to projects like Exxon SABIC plastics plant and desalination facilities

**CC Presence:**  
Active in challenging permits and organizing rallies; e.g., 2024 protests against Inner Harbor desalination plant.

**Threat Assessment:**  
Primarily non-violent; potential for disruption at industrial sites or events supporting energy sector.

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Capability | LOW-MEDIUM (legal/mobilization focus) |
| Intent | MEDIUM (oppose industry-aligned groups) |
| Current Activity | MODERATE |
| Overall Threat | **MODERATE** |

---

### Immigration Rights Organizations

**Overview:**  
Groups focused on immigrant rights, border policies, and opposition to ICE activities. LULAC (League of United Latin American Citizens) has deep roots in Corpus Christi, founded there in 1929.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Advocacy for civil rights, anti-deportation
- Organize protests against raids and policies
- Coalition with national networks for "No Kings" events

**CC Activities:**  
- 2019-2025: Protests against ICE raids and deportation policies
- Participation in large-scale "No Kings" demonstrations (2025)

**Tactics:**
- Marches, rallies, vigils
- Legal advocacy and community education

**Threat Assessment:**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Capability | MEDIUM (mobilization for large events) |
| Intent | LOW-MEDIUM (issue-specific) |
| Current Activity | SPORADIC |
| Overall Threat | **LOW-MODERATE** |

---

### Civil Rights and Social Justice Groups

**Overview:**  
Includes NAACP, Mosaic Project (LGBT rights), and Student Citizen Activists at TAMUCC. Focus on police accountability, LGBTQ+ rights, and racial justice.

**Key Groups:**
- NAACP Corpus Christi: Civil rights advocacy
- Mosaic Project: LGBTQ+ rights
- Student Citizen Activists: Campus-based political activism

**Current Status:**  
Activity peaks with national events (e.g., 2020 BLM protests); otherwise low-key advocacy.

**Tactics:**
- Peaceful demonstrations, community forums
- Social media campaigns

**Threat Assessment:**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Capability | LOW |
| Intent | LOW |
| Current Activity | LOW |
| Overall Threat | **LOW** |

## 3.3 Other Threat Actors

### Lone Actors

Low-probability, high-impact threat amplified by border proximity and military presence (e.g., 2020 NAS shooting).

**Considerations:**
- Monitor for radicalization tied to immigration or environmental issues

### Criminal Opportunists

Large gatherings may attract petty crime; relevant for events near tourist areas like Bayfront.
