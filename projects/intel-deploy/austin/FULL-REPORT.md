## Key Findings

### Threat Environment

The Austin metropolitan area presents a **moderate-to-elevated threat environment** for conservative political organizations. The region is a hub for progressive activism, particularly centered around the University of Texas and the State Capitol, with active involvement from left-wing groups in protests related to social justice, immigration, and anti-police movements.

**Primary Threat Actors:**
- **Palestinian Solidarity Committee (PSC)** — Student group at UT Austin leading pro-Palestinian protests, including encampments and walkouts
- **Students for a Democratic Society (SDS)** — Active in anti-war and free speech demonstrations on campus
- **Red Guard Austin** — Historically significant Maoist group (disbanded in 2022); known for militant tactics and disruptions
- **Party for Socialism and Liberation (PSL) Austin** — Organizes anti-imperialist and pro-Palestine actions; involved in local solidarity protests
- **Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) Austin** — Focuses on labor, electoral organizing, and protests against conservative legislation
- **Various ad-hoc coalitions** — Groups protesting abortion restrictions, trans rights bills, immigration enforcement, and police funding

**Secondary Considerations:**
- High concentration of student activists at UT Austin may lead to rapid mobilization
- State Capitol as a focal point for legislative protests increases potential for confrontations

## Recent History (2020-Present)

### 2020: George Floyd Protests

Protests following George Floyd's murder led to significant unrest in Austin:

- **May-June 2020:** Large demonstrations downtown, at Capitol, and City Hall. Some escalated to vandalism and clashes with APD.
- **Police Response:** Use of less-lethal munitions; multiple injuries reported.
- **Arrests:** Hundreds arrested over the summer.
- **Policy Impact:** Led to defund APD movement and budget reforms.

### 2021-2023: Social Issue Protests

- Abortion rights rallies post-Dobbs (2022)
- Protests against anti-trans legislation (bathroom bills, sports bans)
- Immigration-related demonstrations against state policies

- Over 50 arrests; clashes with state troopers.

- Protest against anti-trans bills during legislative session; disruptions in House chambers.

## 5.1 Protest & Direct Action Tactics

### Standard Protest Tactics

**Marches & Rallies:**
- Organized at Capitol, UT campus, Republic Square
- Chants, signs, speeches
- Often permitted but can go unpermitted

**Civil Disobedience:**
- Sit-ins, die-ins, building occupations
- Blocking entrances to government buildings
- Designed for arrests and media

**Noise Demonstrations:**
- Drums, megaphones outside targeted venues
- Used to disrupt legislative hearings

### Escalated Tactics

**Property Disruption:**
- Graffiti on public buildings
- Vandalism during larger protests
- Rare arson attempts

**Direct Confrontation:**
- Blocking paths, verbal harassment
- Pushing in crowds
- Use of umbrellas, signs as barriers

**Armed Presence:**
- Less common than in other areas, but historical groups like Red Guard carried
- Open carry legal, but rare in protests
