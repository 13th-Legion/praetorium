# Historical Incident Analysis (2020–Present)

## Major Incidents

### 2020 George Floyd Protests — Austin

**Date:** May 29 – June 2020 (peak); continued through summer  
**Location:** Downtown Austin, State Capitol, City Hall, APD Headquarters

**Timeline:**
- **May 30, 2020:** Thousands gathered at APD HQ and Capitol for protests.
- Escalated with clashes; APD used bean bag rounds, injuring protesters.
- **May 31:** Protests spread downtown; some looting and vandalism.
- Curfew imposed; ongoing demonstrations.

**Outcomes:**
- Over 400 arrests.
- Lawsuits against APD for excessive force.
- Led to defund movement and budget cuts.

**Lessons Learned:**
- Rapid escalation in urban core.
- High risk at government buildings.

---

### 2024 UT Austin Pro-Palestinian Encampment

**Date:** April 24-29, 2024  
**Location:** UT Austin South Mall

**Summary:**  
PSC-organized sit-in demanding divestment from Israel-related companies.

**Timeline:**
- **April 24:** Walkout and encampment setup.
- University called state troopers; 57 arrests.
- **April 29:** Second attempt; further arrests and clashes.

**Outcomes:**
- Over 130 total arrests.
- University statements on free speech vs. safety.
- Ongoing litigation.

**Relevance:**  
Demonstrates campus mobilization and police response.

---

### 2025 Legislative Session Protests (Abortion/Trans/Immigration)

**Date:** January-May 2025  
**Location:** Texas State Capitol

**Timeline:**
- **March 2025:** Rallies against anti-trans bills (SB 14, etc.).
- **May 2025:** LGBTQ+ advocates disrupted House sessions.
- Protests on abortion restrictions and immigration enforcement.

**Outcomes:**
- Several arrests for disruptions.
- Some bills passed despite protests.

**Lessons Learned:**
- Capitol as focal point for issue-based protests.

---

### 2025 Anti-ICE Protests

**Date:** June 2025  
**Location:** State Capitol and Downtown Austin

**Summary:**  
Response to federal immigration operations and detainee death.

**Timeline:**
- **June 9-14:** Planned protests; DPS arrests five on June 9, nine on June 14.
- Clashes with police.

**Outcomes:**
- Multiple arrests; highlighted immigration tensions.

---

### Ongoing Defund APD Campaigns (2020-2025)

**Date:** 2020-Present  
**Location:** City Hall, Downtown

**Summary:**  
Push for police budget reforms post-Floyd.

**Key Events:**
- **August 2020:** Council cuts APD budget by ~$150M.
- **2021-2025:** Lawsuits, propositions; staffing shortages.

**Relevance:**  
Creates ongoing tension with law enforcement.

## Incident Pattern Analysis

### Escalation Factors

- **Counter-protests:** Increase confrontation risk.
- **Legislative Triggers:** Bills on social issues spark rallies.
- **Campus Events:** Rapid student mobilization.
- **Police Involvement:** Can lead to clashes.

### Geographic Patterns

| Location Type | Incident Frequency | Severity |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| State Capitol | HIGH | MODERATE-HIGH |
| UT Campus | HIGH | MODERATE |
| City Hall | MODERATE | MODERATE |
| Republic Square | MODERATE | LOW-MODERATE |
| Downtown Streets | LOW-MODERATE | HIGH (when escalated) |

### Seasonal Patterns

- **Spring:** Legislative session peaks.
- **Fall:** Campus activism ramps up.
- **Summer:** Anniversary events.
- **Winter:** Reduced outdoor activity.
