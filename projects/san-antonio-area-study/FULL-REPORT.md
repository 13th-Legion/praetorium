## Key Findings

### Threat Environment

The San Antonio metro area presents a **moderate threat environment** for conservative political organizations. The region has a history of civil unrest, particularly related to police accountability, immigration, and social justice issues, amplified by its proximity to military installations and the U.S.-Mexico border.

**Primary Threat Actors:**
- **DSA San Antonio** — Local chapter of Democratic Socialists of America; focused on labor, housing, and electoral organizing with potential for protest mobilization
- **Black Lives Matter affiliates** — Informal networks active in police oversight campaigns; led major 2020 demonstrations
- **John Brown Gun Club elements** — Armed left-wing presence documented at 2022-2023 events; overlaps with broader activist networks
- **Immigration/anti-ICE activists** — Heightened activity due to border proximity; coordinated with national networks like 50501

**Secondary Considerations:**
- Military base proximity (JBSA installations) may complicate event planning and create unique security dynamics
- SAPD oversight movements (e.g., Fix SAPD) maintain pressure on law enforcement, potentially spilling over to conservative events

## 4.1 Timeline of Key Incidents

### 2020: George Floyd Protests

**May 30, 2020 - Downtown Protest Escalation**  
- Location: Travis Park to Alamo Plaza  
- Description: Initially peaceful rally of ~1,000+ protesting George Floyd's death. Escalated after dark with vandalism, fires, and clashes.  
- Police Response: SAPD deployed tear gas, pepper spray, and kinetic impact projectiles (sponge rounds). Multiple protesters injured, including eye injuries.  
- Arrests: ~50+  
- Relevance: Demonstrated potential for rapid escalation; led to lawsuits and ongoing police reform demands. Armed militia (This Is Texas Freedom Force) present behind police lines.  
- Sources: KSAT, Express-News, TPR  

**June 1-7, 2020 - Sustained Demonstrations**  
- Locations: Various downtown sites, City Hall  
- Description: Daily protests with hundreds to thousands participating; focused on local police accountability.  
- Notable: Peaceful marches, speeches; some property damage.  
- Relevance: Built momentum for SAPD oversight movements like Fix SAPD.  

### 2021-2022: Post-Dobbs and Single-Issue Protests

**June 2022 - Abortion Rights Demonstrations**  
- Location: Downtown, Planned Parenthood sites  
- Description: Protests following Dobbs decision; hundreds rallied against Texas abortion laws.  
- Relevance: Coalition building between DSA, BLM, and feminist groups. Low violence.  

**December 2022 - Drag Show Confrontations**  
- Location: St. Mary's Strip venue  
- Description: Right-wing protesters confronted by armed counter-protesters, including JBGC members.  
- Relevance: Showed armed left-wing presence; tensions but no major violence.  

### 2023-2024: Police Reform and Immigration Focus

**August 2023 - Police Contract Protests**  
- Location: City Hall  
- Description: Activists protested new SAPD union contract, demanding more oversight.  
- Relevance: Fix SAPD-led; highlighted accountability issues. Council approved contract 8-3 despite opposition.  

**Various 2024 - Border-Related Demonstrations**  
- Locations: Near JBSA sites, downtown  
- Description: Small-scale protests against state immigration policies and military involvement.  
- Relevance: Building tension leading into 2025 escalations.  

### 2025: Immigration Crackdown Backlash

**June 11-14, 2025 - "No Kings" Protests**  
- Location: Alamo Plaza, downtown streets  
- Description: Thousands protested Trump administration policies, including mass deportations. Part of national 50501 movement. Peaceful but large-scale; chants against ICE and border militarization.  
- Police/Military Response: National Guard deployed at Alamo; SAPD monitored. No major clashes.  
- Relevance: Largest recent mobilization; highlighted anti-ICE sentiment near border.  

**November 17, 2025 - Anti-Raid Protest**  
- Location: San Pedro Ave & Basse Rd  
- Description: ~100+ protested federal raid detaining 150+ suspected of gang ties. Focused on immigration enforcement tactics.  
- Relevance: Direct response to local ICE/DPS operation; potential for escalation in border-proximate area.
