# Tactics, Techniques & Procedures (TTPs)

## 5.1 Protest & Direct Action Tactics

### Standard Protest Tactics

**Marches & Rallies:**
- Permitted or spontaneous marches in downtown areas
- Rallies at symbolic sites (Alamo Plaza, City Hall, La Villita)
- Signs, chants, megaphones
- Protected under First Amendment

**Civil Disobedience:**
- Street blockades
- Sit-ins at government buildings or raid sites
- Defiance of dispersal orders
- Aimed at media coverage and disruption

**Noise Demonstrations:**
- Drums, horns, pots/pans outside targets
- Used to harass or disrupt events

### Escalated Tactics

**Property Destruction:**
- Vandalism, graffiti
- Window smashing (rare in SA)
- Arson attempts (2020 protests)
- Focused on symbols of authority

**Direct Confrontation:**
- Blocking access to venues
- Mobbing individuals
- Pushing/shoving
- Improvised weapons

**Armed Presence:**
- Legal open carry by JBGC at events
- Defensive positioning
- Pepper spray use documented

## 5.2 Counter-Protest Dynamics

**Phase 1 — Intelligence Gathering:**
- Monitoring conservative event announcements
- Social media reconnaissance
- Venue assessment

**Phase 2 — Mobilization:**
- Calls via Signal, social media
- Coalition with DSA, BLM, immigration groups
- Supply coordination

**Phase 3 — Deployment:**
- Arrival and positioning
- Banners, chanting

**Phase 4 — Confrontation:**
- Access blocking
- Harassment
- Potential clashes if armed

**Phase 5 — Documentation:**
- Filming for doxxing
- Social media amplification
- Follow-up targeting
